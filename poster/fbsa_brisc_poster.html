The task is binary tumor segmentation from BRISC-2025 brain MRI slices.
  • Goal: segment tumor regions accurately while keeping the model interpretable through foreground/background slots.
  • Challenge: tumors vary strongly in shape, size, and contrast across glioma, meningioma, and pituitary cases.
  • Key idea: use DINO ViT patch features, then add slot-based foreground/background grouping before decoding a mask.
Dataset & Evaluation

  • Input: RGB-converted MRI images normalized with ImageNet statistics for DINO.
  • Metrics: Dice, IoU, and Hausdorff distance in pixels.
  • Training: Dice + BCE loss, Adam optimizer, cosine learning rate schedule.
Problem Framing

Plain decoders can achieve strong masks, but they do not expose how features are grouped into foreground and background. Slot attention gives a compact grouping mechanism that can be visualized and inspected.

  • Preserving patch tokens improves Dice by about 17 points over the slot-only decoder.
  • The remaining error is mostly boundary detail, limited by the 14 x 14 ViT patch grid.
Implementation Notes
  • DINO encoder is frozen, reducing trainable parameters and training cost.
  • Slot attention uses two learned slots for foreground/background grouping.
  • Decoder upsamples from 14 x 14 patch features to a 224 x 224 logit mask.

Conclusion
  • Foreground/background slot attention can improve an MRI segmentation decoder when it complements, rather than replaces, DINO patch tokens.
  • The fused model generalizes closely to training performance, suggesting the main gains come from better representation rather than memorization.
  • Future work: add high-resolution skip features, tune thresholding, and compare against a stronger U-Net under matched training settings.
